1860

(“Wide Awake”) Used in the 1860 presidential election within Abraham Lincoln’s campaign. It was within a movement created by the Republican party to promote anti-slavery rhetoric.

1962

“If You’re Woke, You Get It.” Article by novelist, William Melvin Kelley describing inappropriate usage of African American slang in the white community. From 1950 - 2000, “woke” had been coined by popular media to mean someone who is politically informed and cognizant of current political events.

1971

Used in the play Garvey Lives by Barry Beckam. The play is about an African activist known as Marcus Garvey. Line: "I been sleeping all my life. And now that Mr. Garvey done woke me up, I'm gon' stay woke. And I'm gon help him wake up other black folk!”

2008

Used in the song “Master Teacher” by Erykah Badu. Line: “I stay woke.” would later gain association with issues of racial injustice. From the year 2000 to the present, “Woke” was and is now not only a term for political awareness of racial injustice. It also refers to an individual who is constantly alert of racial injustice occurring in their everyday lives.

2013

The term was popularized by the Black Lives Matter movement which made its debut on the internet in July of 2013.

2016

The line “Stay woke.” was used in Childish Gambino’s song, “Redbone”. The use was to encourage political awareness. It is also important to recognize that the title refers to a person who is mixed race.
