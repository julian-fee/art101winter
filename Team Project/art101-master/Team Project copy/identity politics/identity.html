Identity Politics
1970s
The first emergence of the term “identity politics” was first used among philosophers to which debated the extent to which identity played a role in politics.
1980s
Political analysts and historians examined the role which identity politics played in the civil rights movements.
2000s
The phrase “identity politics” began to be used as a pejorative towards political groups who organize and campaign around identity on both sides of the political spectrum. Critics of identity politics say that organizing ideology based around identity encourages division and keeps public discourse inherently divided.
2010
Identity politics is a term that has been used widely for a few decades. It spans multiple political ideologies but mainly focuses on the collective organization of a group, usually based around race, gender, or religion, which focuses on advocating for policies for that particular group.
