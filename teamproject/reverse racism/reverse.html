Reverse Racism

1865-1877
The concept of reverse racism has roots in the 1860s and 70s. This time period, known as the Reconstruction Era, took place after the American Civil War, and whites became concerned that reparations to or advancement of formerly enslaved people would somehow harm them.
1970s
The sentiment grew in response to "color-blind" rhetoric that argued that no one race should be given preferential treatment. Affirmative Action was seen by some white people as a form of discrimination against them.
2020
Reverse racism is viewed by many to be a made up concept, as existing power structures do not allow for such a thing. Some people believe that reverse racism is a catch-all phrase they can use when the color of their skin no longer grants them immunity from facing the consequences of their actions.
